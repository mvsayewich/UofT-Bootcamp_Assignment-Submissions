Analysis of Obesity and Household Income in the United States


On first glance, this graph demonstrates that the lower the mediam household income, the higher percentage of obesity. This implication is driven by the fact that the states with the highest levels of obesity also have the lowest median of income.


The median range in income, is actually teetering toward the higher percentage of obesity. Regardless of geographical location, there is an apparent trend amongst obesity and median income.


The higher median earning brackets are more sparce, but the lowest possible level of obesity on this chart is 21.3%. Event the highest earning bracket of ~$66,500 measures at 27% obesity.
